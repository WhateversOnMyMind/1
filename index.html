Using AI to Combat Wildfires

Using Technological Advancedments to Unravel The Problem We Have Had For Ages

In 2018, a worn suspension hook on a transmission tower broke and dropped a high-voltage powerline onto combustible vegetation, sparking the Camp Fire, an inferno that razed the town of Paradise, California. One rusty hook ended up killing 85 people, destroying more than 18,000 structures, and causing an estimated $16.5 billion in damage. Not only are wildfires dangerous to human life, they are also equally if not more harmful to the Earth we live in by interfering with its carbon cycle.

Now, wildfires run more rampant than ever, espacially because of climate change. Last autumn there were numerous wildfires happening and if you were in the US, you might have even seen it directly.


A startup in the name of ‘Buzz Solutions’ is harnessing technology to act as a force multiplier for grid operators struggling to maintain their transmission and distribution systems in a drier, more combustible post-climate world. Their success will help prevent catastrophic grid failures, reduce utility rates, and protect homeowners from hazards and heartache.

Buzz Solutions employs unique AI algorithms to detect potential fire hazards and maintenance issues in photos of electricity transmission and distribution infrastructure captured by drones, helicopters, and fixed-wing aircraft. When a potential problem is spotted, the relevant image, tagged with GPS coordinates, is forwarded to the client utility's maintenance system, where the threat is analyzed and categorized before a crew is deployed to remedy the issue.
